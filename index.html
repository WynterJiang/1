Project Abstract

This website presents a doctrinal and historical overview of the Restoration of the Gospel of Jesus Christ. Inspired by course content from REL C 225, it aims to blend visual design with spiritual depth. This timeline offers a reflective journey through key events that shaped the Restoration, framed in a modern, reader-friendly digital format.

Purpose: Help users visualize the divine pattern of restoration and understand its continued impact today.

Restoration Timeline

1820 – The First Vision

Joseph Smith sought guidance through sincere prayer and received a vision of God the Father and Jesus Christ, affirming that none of the existing churches were fully true. This moment marked the dawn of the Restoration.

1823 – Angel Moroni Appears

Moroni visited Joseph multiple times, instructing him about the ancient record on gold plates and preparing him for future responsibilities as a prophet.

1829 – Priesthood Restoration

Heavenly messengers conferred divine authority to Joseph and Oliver Cowdery, restoring both the Aaronic and Melchizedek priesthoods to the earth once again.

1830 – Church Organized

With revelation and authority, Joseph Smith formally organized the Church of Jesus Christ on April 6, marking the institutional beginning of the Restoration's earthly phase.

1836 – Kirtland Temple Dedication

Visions and heavenly manifestations occurred, including Christ appearing in the temple. Priesthood keys were restored to further the missionary work and temple ordinances.

Doctrinal and Visual Insights

The Sacred Grove: Pattern of Revelation

Joseph’s First Vision illustrates a model for seeking personal revelation, emphasizing sincerity, humility, and divine response.

The Plates and the Power of Record

The preservation and translation of the gold plates remind us of the Lord’s pattern in preserving sacred records for future generations.

Restoration of Authority

The priesthood restorations not only empowered leaders but opened the path for covenants and ordinances essential for salvation.
